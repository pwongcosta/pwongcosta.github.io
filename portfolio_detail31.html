Daughters Of Paradise

How It Started..

In 2015, a group of friends and I started the Daughters Of Paradise Initiative. Despite our locations being dispersed around the globe, we set our minds to working as a collective of women who were equally passionate about quality design and web-based applications and platforms. Within our first year, we received several awards including a grant which funded our San Francisco tutoring program that eventually took over Girl Code Academy of Applied Computer Sciences to provide affordable private computer science and math tutors for girls between the ages of 8 and 18.Ultimately, it was more than a tutoring program, and instead, a sisterhood and mentoring program for the girls we hoped would follow and even succeed our footsteps. You might be wondering why we painted our logo as a Bird of Paradise. Despite it's magnificence, the flower itself is a symbol of optimism for the future and finding happiness and fulfillment through challenges. This was exactly what we hoped would come from the initiative we formed. I was chosen to handle the brand identity of the collective which ebbed and flowed as our idea of what we wanted to become filled into formation. As a part-time instructor and tutor, I also was in charge of encouraging young girls to build and maintain a passion for the field of computer science with an emphasis in design through managing community projects that encouraged participants to try their hands at making beauitful immersive and interactive web environments. In celebration of the amazing recogition, I took wood boards from a community warehouse and painted both the Girl Code and Daughters Of Paradise emblems which you'll see on both project pages.
